Welcome to my personal academic blog! I'm **Sohag Kumar Saha**, a Ph.D. Researcher in Electrical & Computer Engineering at Tennessee Technological University, and an IEEE Senior Member.

## What to Expect on This Blog

This blog will serve as a space for me to share:

- **Research Insights**: Summaries and explanations of my work on Energy Management Systems, Smart Grids, Battery Energy Storage, and Deep Reinforcement Learning
- **Conference Reports**: Summaries and takeaways from IEEE conferences I attend
- **Tutorials**: Step-by-step guides on MATLAB/Simulink, Typhoon HIL, Python, and other tools I use in my research
- **Career Tips**: Advice for graduate students navigating Ph.D. programs in engineering
- **Technology Thoughts**: My perspective on advances in renewable energy, AI, and smart grid technologies

## My Research Focus

I work at the [Smart Grid Lab](https://www.tntech.edu/cesr/), Center for Energy Systems Research (CESR), under the supervision of Prof. Satish M. Mahajan. My current research involves:

1. **Safe Deep Reinforcement Learning** for Energy Management in Community Microgrids
2. **Consensus Control** for Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS)
3. **Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL)** simulation using Typhoon HIL 606
4. **Solar Irradiance Forecasting** using hybrid deep learning models

## Recent Achievement

I am honored to share that I was elevated to the grade of **IEEE Senior Member** in August 2025 — the highest level of IEEE membership for which one may apply. Only about 10% of nearly half a million IEEE members have achieved this distinction.
